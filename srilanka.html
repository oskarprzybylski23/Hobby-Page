Sri Lanka

Southern Sri Lanka is a haven for travelers seeking a unique and unforgettable adventure. With its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and abundant wildlife, this tropical paradise is a must-visit destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Whether you're interested in surfing, wildlife safaris, or simply lounging on the beach, Southern Sri Lanka has something for everyone.

My trip to Sri Lanka was a dream come true. Unfortunately I only had a couple days planned in this country before continuing my journey and I decided to head south and relax in one of the beach destinations. I started in the beach town of Weligama, where I spent most of my time learning to surf. The waves were perfect for beginners, and the instructors were patient and friendly. I couldn't believe how quickly I progressed and how much fun I had.

In my typical habit, I couldn't stay in one location for too long and I spent most of my time in Sri Lanka driving a scooter along the coast, discovering numerous beaches. Each one was more beautiful than the last, with crystal-clear water and soft, white sand. But my favorite beach was Harikatya, where I spent an entire day surfing and relaxing. The waves were perfect, and the beach was quiet and secluded, with only a few other surfers in the water.

When I wasn't surfing or relaxing on the beach, I explored the nearby towns and villages. I visited Galle, a historic Dutch fort city with charming streets lined with colorful buildings and cafes. I also went to Mirissa, a small fishing village with a laid-back vibe and stunning views of the ocean. The people in Southern Sri Lanka were incredibly welcoming and friendly, and I felt right at home.

The beaches, the people, and the culture were all amazing, and I know I will return. If you're looking for a tropical paradise with great surfing, beautiful scenery, and friendly locals, I highly recommend a visit to Southern Sri Lanka and I will definetely come back to discover other parts of this beautiful country.
